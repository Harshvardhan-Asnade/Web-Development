Iron Man

by Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, Don Heck, and Jack Kirby

Iron Man (Tony Stark) is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character was created by writer Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, artist Don Heck, and developed by Jack Kirby. Iron Man first appeared in Tales of Suspense #39 (cover dated March 1963).

Tony Stark is a wealthy industrialist and genius inventor. After suffering a severe chest injury during a kidnapping, Stark is forced to build a suit of armor to save his life and escape captivity. The experience transforms him into Iron Man, and he uses his newfound abilities to fight evil.

Over time, Stark evolves into a founding member of the Avengers, a group of superheroes who fight against the most dangerous threats to Earth.

Iron Man's story reflects themes of redemption, the balance of power and responsibility, and the constant struggle between his personal life and his duties as a hero.
